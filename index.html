Manny "Pac-Man" Pacquiao

Manny Pacquiao's life

  • December 1978 - Born in Kibawe, Bukidnon
  • January 1995 - Made his professional debut as a junior flyweight
  • December 1998 - Won his first major title the World Boxing Council (WBC) flyweight title
  • September 1999 - Lost the title to Medgoen Singsurat
  • June 2001 - His US debut as he moves up in weight class, scored a sixth-round knockout of Lehlo Ledwaba to win the International Boxing Federation (IBF) junior featherweight title
  • 2003 - Voted as Person of the Year in the Philippines over Gloria Macapagal Arroyo
  • November 2003 - Become The Ring magazine featherweight champion by defeating Antonio Barrera
  • December 2008 - Faced Oscar De La Hoya in a celebrated nontitle welterweight
  • 2009 - Formed a new political party, the Peoples’ Champ Movement
  • May 2009 - Won The Ring’s junior welterweight championship with a spectacular one-punch second-round knockout to Ricky Hatton
  • November 2009 - defeated Miguel Cotto in 12 rounds to take the World Boxing Organization (WBO) welterweight title
  • March 2010 - Defended the title by defeating Joshua Clottey
  • May 2010 - Won for a legislative seat, for a district in Mindanao
  • November 2010 - Defeated WBC super welterweight champion Antonio Margarito
  • June 2012 - His 15-bout winning streak ended with the defeat to Timothy Bradley and lost his WBO Weltherweight Title
  • December 2012 - Lost a non-title bout to Juan Manuel Márquez
  • 2013 - Got Re-elected to the position
  • April 2014 - Regained the WBO welterweight belt by defeating Timothy Bradley
  • May 2015 - Lost to Floyd Mayweather by unanimous decision
  • May 2016 - Elected as the Senator of the Philippines
  • April 2016 - Announced his retirement after the win against Timothy Bradley
  • July 2017 - Lost his WBO Weltherweight title to Jeff Horn
  • July 2018 - Captured the WBA welterweight title in a victory over Lucas Matthysse
  • July 2019 - won a split decision over the previously undefeated Keith Thurman to take the WBA super welterweight belt and grabbed the title of "Oldest welterweight champion in boxing history"

"This is not about my comeback. My victory is a symbol of my people’s comeback from a natural disaster and a national tragedy.."

-- Senator of the Philippines Manny Pacquiao
